Dodge Challenger Overview

The Dodge Challenger is an iconic American muscle car, blending retro-inspired styling with powerful performance. Initially released in 1970, the Challenger was Dodgeâ€™s entry into the "pony car" market, competing with the Ford Mustang and Chevrolet Camaro. The modern Challenger, reintroduced in 2008, pays homage to its first-generation roots with bold design elements like a long hood and split grille. It offers a variety of powertrains, from a 3.6L V6 to supercharged 6.2L HEMI V8 engines, producing up to 840 horsepower in the limited-edition SRT Demon. The Challenger's interior combines classic and modern features, including the Uconnect system and advanced safety options. Known for straight-line speed and track-ready performance, the Challenger also offers special editions like the Hellcat and Widebody. With its customizability and powerful presence, the Dodge Challenger is a symbol of American muscle and innovation.
